The core idea

A slow brain and a fast reflex, working together.

Project Zomboid is real-time — a zombie kills you in the second it takes to reach you, and an LLM takes several seconds to answer. So control is split across two layers running at two very different speeds.

EVERY ~5s

The Brain — strategy

An LLM reads a live feed of the world and decides intent: where to go, what to loot, when to hide, what it's feeling. It never micromanages a fight — it decides “clear that house, then hole up,” and hands off.

latency: seconds · runs in the companion bridge

EVERY FEW TICKS

The Reflex — survival

Deterministic Lua running on the game's own clock. It reacts the same tick a threat appears: flee a swarm along a repulsion vector, stand and fight a lone attacker, close doors, dodge — no round-trip to the brain.

latency: instant · runs inside the game
What it does

Everything it perceives and decides, on screen.

Transparency is the point — you watch a mind survive, you don't just watch a character move.

Real LLM control

Every strategic choice is genuinely reasoned turn by turn from the live game state — not a decision tree.

Fight or flee

Armed and not overwhelmed, it closes in and swings. Unarmed, swarmed, or hurt, it breaks away. Decided locally, instantly.

Live inner monologue

An in-game HUD shows its current action, first-person thoughts, vitals and threats. It speaks decisions aloud.

Loot · conceal · break in

Grabs food, water and weapons (and says what it took), closes doors and curtains to hide, smashes windows into locked buildings.

You can take over

Grab the keyboard any time to save it; the AI stands down and auto-resumes ten seconds after you stop.

Smart game-speed

Auto fast-forwards safe travel, snaps to normal near danger, and can pause for a beat when things get truly dire.

Bring your own AI

Runs on any model — free local, or hosted.

The brain is backend-agnostic. Point it at a free local model, or any major provider. It auto-detects from whatever key you set.

Get started

Three steps, about five minutes.

You need Project Zomboid (Build 42), Node.js 18+, and one AI backend from above.

Install the mod

Drop mods/ClaudeSurvivor into your Zomboid mods folder (or run the helper).

pwsh ./install.ps1

Start the brain

Launch the bridge with your chosen backend. It serves the live dashboard.

cd bridge
node pz-bridge.mjs

Let go

Enable Claude Survivor in-game, start a Sandbox run, and take your hands off the keyboard.

# then just watch:
localhost:8799/mind
Fair warning: most runs end badly. It's a zombie apocalypse, survival quality is a work in progress, and that's the fun — every run is a different improvised story. Jump in when you can't watch it die.
